World Cafe®

Hosted by Raina Douris · Produced by WXPN · Distributed by NPR

World Cafe is NPR's most popular music program. Presenting an eclectic mix from both heritage and up-and-coming artists, the Cafe takes over a half million listeners on a journey of musical discovery each week. This two-hour daily program is nationally syndicated on over 300 stations in markets including New York, Chicago, Austin, Seattle, Boston, Nashville, St. Louis, Atlanta, and Philadelphia.

Hosted by RAINA DOURIS, each episode of World Cafe is anchored by a live performance and intimate interview with a musical guest.

Over the course of 30 years, the Cafe has featured a wide range of guests, including: PAUL MCCARTNEY, LIZZO, METALLICA, BARBRA STREISAND, CARLOS SANTANA, BILLIE EILISH, THE B-52'S, BUDDY GUY, TEARS FOR FEARS, ELTON JOHN, QUESTLOVE, ADELE, TOWNES VAN ZANDT, DUMPSTAPHUNK, DAVID BOWIE, MANDY MOORE, DOLLY PARTON, COLDPLAY, BOOTSY COLLINS, JONI MITCHELL, ALABAMA SHAKES, AL GREEN, DEMI LOVATO, TOM JONES, MACHINE GUN KELLY, SAM SMITH, BRANDI CARLILE, and BRUCE SPRINGSTEEN, among thousands of others.

World Cafe periodically delivers topical segments on music-related books, movies, and TV shows. Past segments have featured interviews with actors, directors, and composers from This Is Us, Glee, O Brother Where Art Thou, and Summer Of Soul, while artists like DAVE GROHL, KENNY LOGGINS, and BILLY IDOL have discussed their memoirs.

World Cafe's on-air team is rounded out by contributing host STEPHEN KALLAO, while JOHN MORRISON, JESSIE SCOTT, and GWEN THOMPKINS are frequent correspondents spotlighting a variety of cultural and regional music topics.

Conversations From The World Cafe is a weekly one-hour radio magazine that presents some interview and performance elements of the daily show, while exploring issues and trends in contemporary music and culture.

The rising popularity of World Cafe inspired the creation of the state-of-the-art restaurant/performance venue, World Cafe Live, which is independently owned and operated.

World Cafe is produced by WXPN, the public radio service of the University of Pennsylvania, and distributed nationally by NPR. The program's website is www.WorldCafe.org, where thousands of episode archives are available to stream on-demand.
