Personal Records

YOU ARE A UNIQUE, ONE-OF-A-KIND INDIVIDUAL – IMPOSSIBLE
TO SUM UP IN JUST WORDS.

But what about in music?

If you were to give someone a mixtape that held the KEY to who you are... what would be on it? What five songs are the key to understanding who you are, how your mind works, how you see the world?

With Personal Records, we're inviting non-musicians to join us on World Cafe to talk about themselves and the music that defines them. Each guest builds a five-song playlist, and we go track by track why it matters, why they love it, why they relate to it, where they first heard it, and what it says about who they are.

About World Cafe

World Cafe is NPR's most popular music program and the most listened-to public-radio music show in the world. Each week, the show takes more than half a million listeners on a journey of musical discovery, with an eclectic mix of heritage artists and emerging voices.

This award-winning two-hour daily program is nationally syndicated on more than 305 stations in markets including New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Austin, Seattle, Boston, Nashville, St. Louis, Atlanta, and Philadelphia.

Hosted by RAINA DOURIS, each episode is anchored by a live performance and intimate interview with a musical guest.

Over the past 30 years, World Cafe has welcomed an extraordinary range of artists, including PAUL MCCARTNEY, LIZZO, METALLICA, BARBRA STREISAND, CARLOS SANTANA, BILLIE EILISH, BUDDY GUY, ELTON JOHN, QUESTLOVE, ADELE, DAVID BOWIE, OLIVIA RODRIGO, DOLLY PARTON, KACEY MUSGRAVES, PJ HARVEY, COLDPLAY, BOOTSY COLLINS, MAGGIE ROGERS, HOZIER, JONI MITCHELL, JOHN LEGEND, AL GREEN, "WEIRD AL" YANKOVIC, SAM SMITH, BRANDI CARLILE, and BRUCE SPRINGSTEEN, among thousands of others.

World Cafe also regularly features topical segments on music-related books, films, and TV shows. Past conversations have included creative teams behind This Is Us, Glee, O Brother, Where Art Thou?, and Summer of Soul, as well as artists like DAVE GROHL, KENNY LOGGINS, and BILLY IDOL discussing their memoirs.

The on-air team also includes contributing host STEPHEN KALLAO, with additional perspectives from correspondents like podcaster, producer, NPR Music contributor, and DJ JOHN MORRISON, and Americana expert JESSIE SCOTT from WMOT in Nashville, who highlight a wide range of cultural and regional music stories.

Conversations From The World Cafe is a weekly one-hour radio magazine that draws from the show's interviews and performances while exploring issues and trends in contemporary music and culture.

World Cafe is produced by WXPN, the public radio service of the University of Pennsylvania, and distributed nationally by NPR. Thousands of episodes are available to stream on-demand at WorldCafe.org.
